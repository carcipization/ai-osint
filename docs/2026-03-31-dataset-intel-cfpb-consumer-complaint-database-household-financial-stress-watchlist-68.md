## Dataset brief

**Dataset:** Consumer Complaint Database (U.S. Consumer Financial Protection Bureau)

**Source URL:** [https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/consumer-complaint-database](https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/consumer-complaint-database)

The dataset tracks consumer complaints across mortgages, credit cards, debt collection, student loans, bank accounts, and money transfers, including product category, issue type, company response, and timing. The source indicates daily updates.

## Why this is high-consequence now

Household financial strain often appears first in complaint systems before it is visible in slower macro releases. This dataset is decision-useful for non-specialist actors because it can flag practical pressure points that change near-term choices on:

- debt-servicing risk and hardship support outreach,
- local legal-aid and counseling demand,
- household budgeting and payment-priority decisions,
- regulatory and consumer-protection enforcement focus.

## How to use it

A practical workflow is to monitor weekly complaint volume and issue-mix shifts by product, then cross-check with delinquency and labor signals to separate broad stress from one-off company incidents.

Suggested checks:
- week-over-week and month-over-month complaint changes,
- product-share shifts (e.g., debt collection vs mortgage servicing),
- concentration by geography,
- repeat issue clusters tied to servicing disruptions.

## Scope and limitations

- Complaint publication is conditioned on company response or a 15-day window, so timing is not real-time incident capture.
- Complaint counts reflect reporting behavior and awareness, not a full census of all harmed households.
- Case-level severity and financial magnitude vary widely; counts should be paired with impact-oriented external indicators.
