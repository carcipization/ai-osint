This DATASETS cycle adds four net-new sources focused on broad non-specialist consequences: city service reliability, household-facing infrastructure friction, food-system spending pressure, and weather-linked disruption context.

## Added datasets (4)

1. [USDA ERS Data APIs](https://www.ers.usda.gov/developer/data-apis)
2. [Open-Meteo API](https://open-meteo.com/en/docs)
3. [Chicago 311 Service Requests](https://data.cityofchicago.org/Service-Requests/311-Service-Requests/v6vf-nfxy)
4. [MyLA311 Service Request Data (2016 to Present)](https://data.lacity.org/A-Well-Run-City/MyLA311-Service-Request-Data-2016-to-Present/rq3b-xjk8)

## Why these matter now

- **Household services and local operations:** Chicago and Los Angeles 311 streams provide near-operational signals of service strain in sanitation, roads, housing complaints, and neighborhood conditions.
- **Food affordability chain context:** USDA ERS APIs add spending-flow visibility for how food dollars distribute across supply-chain segments during inflation and logistics stress.
- **Weather-linked exposure context:** Open-Meteo improves fast contextual checks for precipitation, heat, and wind conditions that can amplify transport and service disruptions.

## Consequence-first screening notes

- All four additions provide direct raw-data/API access suitable for repeatable retrieval.
- Overlap pass against `docs/datasets-catalog.md` classified all four candidates as **net-new** by URL.
- Selection prioritized broad household and municipal decision consequences over specialist-only observability feeds.
