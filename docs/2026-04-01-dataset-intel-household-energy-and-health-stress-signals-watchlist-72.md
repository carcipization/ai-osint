This DATASETS_A cycle adds three public datasets focused on household stability under combined energy-cost and health-capacity pressure.

## Added in this batch (3 net-new)

1. **Weatherization Assistance Program and Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program for Solar Survey Responses and Plan**  
   Multi-year LIHEAP/WAP administrator survey and plan-coding data for monitoring how low-income energy-support systems are adapting to household affordability and resilience stress.

2. **Mental Health Care in the Last 4 Weeks**  
   Household Pulse-derived estimates on recent mental-health care access/use as a fast signal of care friction during social and economic strain.

3. **Post-COVID Conditions**  
   Household Pulse-linked long-COVID prevalence estimates that help track persistent health constraints with direct spillovers into work capacity and household stability.

## Why this matters now

Energy affordability and health access pressures often compound rather than occur in isolation. This batch improves monitoring of:
- whether low-income energy-assistance systems are adapting fast enough,
- whether recent mental-health care access is tightening,
- and whether persistent post-COVID burden remains a drag on household and labor resilience.

These are practical early-warning signals for non-specialist consequences (utility insecurity, reduced work continuity, and rising local support demand).
