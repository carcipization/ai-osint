What this dataset is

A U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA) dataset providing importer-level petroleum import records, including company identity, product categories, and import volumes by reporting period.

Scope and limitations

  • Import data can carry release lag and revisions; it is not a real-time tanker tracker.
  • Company-level reporting can reflect corporate structure changes and filing corrections over time.
  • Use with refinery runs, stock levels, and retail price series for full pass-through assessment.

How to use it

  1. Track concentration: identify whether import dependence is clustering in a small set of firms or source routes.
  2. Detect substitution: compare product mix shifts (e.g., crude vs refined products) during disruption windows.
  3. Link to consumer outcomes: pair importer shifts with diesel/gasoline retail datasets and freight indicators to estimate household and logistics cost pressure.

Why this matters now

When export terminals and shipping corridors are repeatedly disrupted, importer-level fuel flow data helps households, businesses, and local planners understand whether supply risk is spreading or stabilizing. That supports concrete decisions on procurement timing, contingency stocks, route planning, and budgeting for fuel-sensitive operations.
