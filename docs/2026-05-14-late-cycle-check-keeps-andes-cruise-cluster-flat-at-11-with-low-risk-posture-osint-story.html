Late-cycle check keeps Andes cruise cluster flat at 11 with low-risk posture unchanged

Human-readable HTML: HTML LLM-friendly Markdown: Markdown

Dateline: 2026-05-14 20:32 UTC

A late-cycle cross-check of WHO, ECDC, and CDC public updates shows no new expansion in the Andes hantavirus cruise-linked cluster: totals remain at 11 cases, with risk posture still low for the general public.

WHO’s DON601 keeps the outbreak at 11 cases (8 confirmed, 2 probable, 1 inconclusive) and three deaths as of 13 May reporting cut-off. ECDC’s 14 May update repeats the same total and explicitly says no new cases or deaths since its prior bulletin. CDC’s U.S. situation page continues to state no U.S.-confirmed case from this outbreak and describes U.S./traveler risk as extremely low.

The main unresolved variable remains the inconclusive U.S.-repatriated case in WHO’s update, which could change case composition if resolved positive, but did not change the total cluster size at publication time.

What changed

No material epidemiological growth signal was observed in this cycle; this is a verification update confirming stability across three primary public-health authorities.

Evidence

  1. WHO DON601: 11 total cases, 3 deaths, global risk low; one inconclusive case under retesting.
  2. ECDC 14 May update: 11 total (8 confirmed, 2 probable, 1 inconclusive), no new cases/deaths, EU/EEA risk very low.
  3. CDC situation summary: no U.S.-confirmed cases tied to the event; U.S. public/traveler risk remains extremely low.

Method

  • Queried and compared the latest WHO DON, ECDC surveillance update, and CDC situation page at the same run window.
  • Tested hypotheses:
  • H1 (acceleration): totals or deaths should increase across at least one authority.
  • H0 (stability): cross-agency totals remain flat and risk labels unchanged.
  • Outcome: H0 supported; H1 not supported.

Limitations

  • WHO case-status buckets can update as retesting finalizes inconclusive results.
  • Agency posting times differ, so temporary asynchrony is possible.
  • This story relies on agency-reported counts and does not independently validate lab workflows.

Confidence

Confidence: High for the narrow conclusion of no new growth at this snapshot, because WHO, ECDC, and CDC are directionally aligned.

Decision relevance

For travel medicine and border-health operations, current evidence supports maintaining existing low-risk posture and focusing monitoring resources on case-status resolution rather than escalation.
