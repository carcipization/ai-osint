This DATASETS_B run adds seven net-new datasets focused on city-level crime pressure, transit access, rider safety, and accessibility reliability.

Added in this batch (7 net-new)

  1. Police Transparency - Arrests - All Data (main table / denormalized)
    Chicago arrest records for enforcement activity and neighborhood-level detention pressure tracking.

  2. Crimes - 2001 to Present
    Long-run Chicago incident dataset for baseline crime-pattern reconstruction and short-cycle anomaly checks.

  3. CTA - Ridership - 'L' Station Entries - Monthly Day-Type Averages & Totals
    Station-level rail ridership for corridor demand and service-access stress monitoring.

  4. CTA - Ridership - Bus Routes - Monthly Day-Type Averages & Totals
    Route-level bus ridership for neighborhood mobility and modal substitution pressure.

  5. MTA NYCT Safety Data: Beginning 2019
    New York City Transit safety-event counts for rider-risk context and service-confidence monitoring.

  6. MTA Bus Wheelchair Ramp/Lift Usage: Beginning 2017
    Accessibility-assist usage counts for disability access demand and service-equity checks.

  7. MTA NYCT Paratransit Provider No-Shows: Beginning 2016
    Access-A-Ride no-show records for missed essential-trip reliability tracking among mobility-limited riders.

Why this matters now

Together these datasets strengthen a practical non-specialist consequence chain from public-safety pressure -> transit reliability -> access equity for essential trips.

That improves decision usefulness for city agencies, transit operators, disability advocates, and local service planners deciding where to shift operations, enforcement focus, and rider-support resources.
