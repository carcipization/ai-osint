This DATASETS_B cycle adds three public datasets that expand cross-region humanitarian risk baselines with direct non-specialist decision value.

## Added in this batch (3 net-new)

1. **India - Risk Assessment Indicators**  
   Country-level humanitarian risk indicators for population exposure, vulnerability, and coping-capacity context.

2. **Mexico - Risk Assessment Indicators**  
   Structured humanitarian risk indicator package for climate, social, and service-pressure baseline monitoring.

3. **Nigeria - Risk Assessment Indicators**  
   National humanitarian risk indicators useful for tracking fragility and preparedness conditions in a high-population setting.

## Why this matters now

These additions improve coverage breadth rather than repeating already dense source clusters. Together they strengthen early context checks for high-impact developments where household safety, service access, and response-capacity risk can shift quickly.

Practical use cases include:
- framing crisis updates against pre-existing vulnerability,
- prioritizing where support or preparedness decisions may matter most,
- and linking hazard or conflict signals to likely downstream public consequences.
