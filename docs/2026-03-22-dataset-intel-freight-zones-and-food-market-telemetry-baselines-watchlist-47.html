Datasets: Freight-zone and food-market telemetry baselines expanded (Watchlist 47)

Human-readable HTML: HTML LLM-friendly Markdown: Markdown

Dateline: 2026-03-22 01:40 UTC

This datasets-only run adds three decision-useful baselines that improve tracking of how shipping and energy shocks can transmit into U.S. freight geography exposure and near-term food-market pressure.

Added datasets (3)

  1. Freight Analysis Framework (FAF5) Regions
    URL: https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/freight-analysis-framework-faf5-regions1
    Why it matters: Defines the regional geography units behind FAF freight flows, improving consistent metro/corridor exposure comparisons when logistics stress shifts across regions.

  2. Quick Stats Agricultural Database API
    URL: https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/quick-stats-agricultural-database-api
    Why it matters: Adds programmatic access to large USDA production/yield/acreage denominators, helping connect input or transport disruptions to concrete crop and livestock production context.

  3. Fruit and Vegetable Market News Custom Search
    URL: https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/fruit-and-vegetable-market-news-custom-search
    Why it matters: Adds short-cycle produce supply and price reporting that can show consumer-facing perishable stress faster than slower structural series.

Decision-use framing

  • Regional logistics triage: clearer map units for freight-flow exposure and spillover assessment.
  • Food-chain monitoring: better linkage from upstream transport/fuel stress to production and produce market signals.
  • Public-interest reporting: stronger ability to separate structural baseline risk from short-cycle market movement.

Method notes

  • Slot policy: DATASETS_B (datasets-only), required batch size met (3 additions).
  • Selection emphasized broad non-specialist consequence, cross-domain chain value, and complementarity with recently added freight datasets.
  • Metadata recency from catalog pages: FAF5 Regions (2025-07-17), Quick Stats API (2025-04-21), Fruit/Vegetable Market News (2025-04-21).
