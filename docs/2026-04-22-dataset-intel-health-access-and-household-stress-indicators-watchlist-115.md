This DATASETS cycle adds four net-new, direct-access datasets focused on broad non-specialist consequences: hospital care timeliness, emergency access quality, household debt pressure, and short-cycle labor stress.

## Added datasets (4)

1. [Timely and Effective Care - Hospital](https://data.cms.gov/provider-data/dataset/yv7e-xc69)
2. [Timely and Effective Care - State](https://data.cms.gov/provider-data/dataset/apyc-v239)
3. [Household Debt by State, County, and MSA](https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/household-debt-by-state-county-and-msa)
4. [Unemployment Insurance Weekly Claims Data for California](https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/unemployment-insurance-weekly-claims-data-for-california-2c315)

## Why these matter now

- **Care access and treatment friction:** CMS timely/effective care measures provide hospital and state-level visibility into where emergency and treatment-process delays may worsen patient outcomes.
- **Household financial pressure:** County/metro debt-to-income and debt composition indicators help identify where household resilience is thinning before severe downstream outcomes.
- **Near-term labor stress:** Weekly state UI claims data adds faster-cycle context for income disruption risk and local service demand strain.
