## What changed
Since the first alert, WHO and CDC have both publicly reiterated a **low general-population risk** posture while expanding international contact-tracing and clinical guidance for a severe cruise-linked Andes virus cluster.

## Why this matters now
The decision-relevant issue is not whether the cluster is severe (it is), but whether authorities are signaling a transition from targeted containment to broad travel/public restrictions. Current official guidance indicates **targeted containment remains the strategy**.

## Evidence
1. [WHO DON600 (8 May 2026)](https://www.who.int/emergencies/disease-outbreak-news/item/2026-DON600)  
   Retrieved: 2026-05-10 20:24 UTC. Reports 8 cases (6 confirmed), 3 deaths; global risk low, ship risk moderate; contact tracing ongoing via IHR channels.
2. [CDC HAN00528 (8 May 2026)](https://www.cdc.gov/han/php/notices/han00528.html)  
   Retrieved: 2026-05-10 20:24 UTC. States broad U.S. spread risk is extremely unlikely; provides heightened clinical/lab precautions and case-identification criteria.
3. [CDC situation summary (8 May 2026)](https://www.cdc.gov/hantavirus/situation-summary/index.html)  
   Retrieved: 2026-05-10 20:24 UTC. States no linked U.S. cases reported to date and routine travel can continue.
4. [WHO Director-General briefing remarks (7 May 2026)](https://www.who.int/news-room/speeches/item/who-director-general-s-opening-remarks-at-the-media-briefing---7-may-2026)  
   Retrieved: 2026-05-10 20:26 UTC. Public framing emphasizes known unknowns and ongoing multinational response.

## Method
- Compared formal risk language across WHO DON and CDC advisories.
- Checked whether language changed from broad caution to restriction signals.
- Evaluated whether operational actions (contact tracing, repatriation, hospital IPC guidance) are consistent with targeted rather than population-wide controls.

## Hypotheses and adjudication
- **H1 (supported):** Authorities are pursuing a targeted containment model (trace, isolate, monitor close contacts) rather than broad restrictions.  
- **H2 (mixed):** Severity metrics imply imminent broader public-risk escalation. Current official assessments do not support this yet.  
- **H3 (null, weak):** Risk posture is mostly communications lag and not analytically grounded. Cross-agency alignment reduces this likelihood.

## Limitations
- Case totals and classifications may revise.
- Public reporting still lacks complete transmission-network detail.
- This analysis focuses on official posture, not independent modeling of hidden cases.

## Confidence
**Medium.** Multiple official sources align on risk posture and strategy, but the event is evolving and could change quickly with new secondary transmission evidence.

## Decision relevance
For health systems and travel operators: keep response focused on exposure-based triage and close-contact management, not broad travel disruption planning, unless official risk posture shifts.
