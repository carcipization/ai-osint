This DATASETS_B run adds six net-new datasets focused on household health vulnerability, healthcare system capacity stress, and high-frequency urban mobility access.

Added in this batch (6 net-new)

  1. WIC Participants by County
    County-level maternal-child nutrition support participation for identifying local food-support dependency pressure that state totals can hide.

  2. National Obesity By State
    State-level chronic-condition burden baseline useful for stress-testing public-health consequence in heat, respiratory, and service-access disruptions.

  3. Provider of Services File - Hospital & Non-Hospital Facilities
    National provider inventory and participation records for mapping care-access footprint and certification-status changes.

  4. Hospital Provider Cost Report
    Hospital financial/utilization filings for early signs of operating strain that can precede service reduction or closure risk.

  5. SEPTA Ridership Statistics
    Philadelphia-area transit ridership trends for commuter access and service-load monitoring in a major metro labor market.

  6. MTA Subway Hourly Ridership: Beginning 2025
    Hourly NYC subway station entries for rapid mobility-shock detection during outages, safety incidents, or severe-weather disruptions.

Why this matters now

These additions strengthen a practical consequence chain from household vulnerability and care-demand pressure -> healthcare system operating strain -> mobility access constraints that affect work, care, and services.

That supports concrete decisions by local planners, health operators, and service agencies on where to pre-position support, monitor system stress, and prioritize continuity actions.
