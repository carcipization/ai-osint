This DATASETS_A cycle adds three public datasets focused on near-term household instability signals: disaster-driven rental displacement risk, emergency cash-assistance demand, and utility-service complaint pressure.

Added in this batch (3 net-new)

  1. Housing Assistance Program Data - Renters (FEMA / NEMIS)
    Open-disaster renter assistance records for tracking displacement pressure and temporary-housing demand after declared disasters.

  2. Cash Assistance Emergency Assistance Requests (NYC DSS/HRA)
    Quarterly emergency-grant request statistics (including rent-arrears related requests) for detecting acute household cash shortfalls.

  3. Public Utility Commission of Texas - Informal Complaints
    Utility complaint records across electric, water, sewer, and telecom services for early warning on service-access and billing friction.

Why this matters now

Household stress often appears first in administrative service systems before it is obvious in slower national indicators. This stack improves practical monitoring of: - post-disaster rental and shelter pressure, - emergency cash-assistance demand spikes, - utility affordability and service-quality friction affecting daily life.

These signals are decision-useful for local governments, legal-aid networks, social-service operators, and reporters prioritizing rapid hardship-response coverage.
