This DATASETS_A run adds four federal health-access datasets that improve non-specialist consequence coverage across one practical chain: insurance coverage shifts -> medicine affordability pressure -> infectious-disease burden -> treatment access.

Added datasets (new this run)

  1. Medicare Monthly Enrollment
    Why it matters now: monthly county/state enrollment shifts can flag coverage churn that changes appointment, prescription, and care-planning behavior.

  2. NADAC (National Average Drug Acquisition Cost) 2026
    Why it matters now: weekly pharmacy acquisition-cost reference data helps detect medicine price-pressure movement sooner than slower annual summaries.

  3. NNDSS Weekly Data
    Why it matters now: provisional weekly notifiable-disease counts provide a fast signal for community disease pressure that can affect school/workplace and care-demand decisions.

  4. ASPR Treatments Locator
    Why it matters now: location-level therapeutic availability signals can expose local treatment-access friction during respiratory disease waves.
