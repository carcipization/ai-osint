This DATASETS_B cycle adds three country-level humanitarian risk datasets that broaden coverage for high-population, high-consequence monitoring contexts.

## Added in this batch (3 net-new)

1. **Pakistan - Risk Assessment Indicators**  
   Country-level humanitarian risk indicators for exposure, vulnerability, and preparedness context in a climate- and displacement-sensitive region.

2. **Ethiopia - Risk Assessment Indicators**  
   Structured humanitarian risk indicators supporting baseline checks in a country with recurrent drought, food-security strain, and displacement pressures.

3. **Indonesia - Risk Assessment Indicators**  
   National humanitarian risk indicators useful for linking hazard exposure and service-capacity stress in a large archipelagic population.

## Why this matters now

These additions improve breadth across South Asia, the Horn of Africa, and Southeast Asia, which strengthens cross-region consequence screening when major weather, conflict, or service-disruption signals emerge.

Practical use cases include:
- adding rapid vulnerability context to breaking hazard coverage,
- prioritizing where population exposure could translate into service-access stress,
- and improving cross-country denominator checks before elevating story candidates.
