This DATASETS_A cycle adds five public datasets that improve consequence-first monitoring across three systems non-specialists feel quickly: child nutrition coverage, low-income energy affordability, and day-to-day transit access. Together, they strengthen early warning on when household cost pressure can convert into service loss or mobility disruption.

## Added in this batch (5 net-new)

### Child nutrition and household food-security pressure
- **National School Lunch Assistance Program Participation and Meals Served Data** — monthly/annual participation and meals-served series for school lunch dependence tracking.
- **School Breakfast Participation and Meals Served Data** — breakfast participation and assistance series for morning food-access and student readiness pressure.
- **Summer Food Service Participation, Meals, and Costs Data** — summer-meal program participation and payment records for out-of-school nutrition gap monitoring.

### Household utility-cost burden
- **Department of Energy - Low-Income Energy Affordability Data (LEAD Tool) - 2022 Update** — modeled low- and moderate-income energy burden indicators by geography/fuel type.

### Daily mobility access reliability
- **Daily Transit Ridership** — daily ridership counts for major U.S. systems (including MTA, BART, WMATA) for faster commuting-access stress checks.

## Why this matters now

These datasets strengthen coverage of household consequence chains that are often missed when only macro indicators are tracked:
- school meal datasets show where food-security strain may intensify as budgets tighten,
- LEAD identifies where energy burden is already high enough that price shocks can cascade into arrears or service insecurity,
- daily transit ridership provides faster evidence of mobility stress than monthly transit releases.

For non-specialists and operators, this supports practical decisions: where to prioritize nutrition outreach, where utility-assistance pressure is likely to rise, and where commuting reliability deterioration may require schedule or staffing adjustments.
