Dateline: 2026-05-13 02:28 UTC

CDC’s latest situation page now emphasizes that exposed U.S. passengers have already been repatriated to high-containment treatment sites and are under public-health monitoring, marking an operational transition from outbound deployment to domestic follow-through. The risk framing remains unchanged: extremely low for the U.S. public and travelers.

What changed

Earlier updates focused on deployment and planned repatriation. The newest CDC language states repatriation has been completed and that exposed passengers are being monitored by health departments.

Limitations
