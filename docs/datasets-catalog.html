  1. Preserve section-level taxonomy unless a split/merge clearly improves retrieval speed.
  2. Prefer editing descriptors over moving entries across sections.
  3. Keep each entry to one sentence of scope + one sentence of caveat/value.
  4. If adding aliases in future, keep one canonical entry and mention aliases in-text.
  5. Re-run duplicate-domain and section-balance checks before publish.
  6. For entries used in current-cycle analysis, surface revision/provisional flags in the story method/limitations when the source exposes them.
  7. For city-level incident feeds, prefer one canonical entry per city/system and avoid near-duplicate mirrors unless they add distinct fields.
  8. For catalog entries with date-stamped titles (e.g., corridor snapshots), keep the version date in the title and note supersession risk in the descriptor.
  9. For country-sharded datasets (same schema repeated across countries), prefer one concise naming pattern and keep descriptors template-consistent so retrieval and dedup checks remain stable.
  10. When adding multiple country shards in one cycle, record the family label in traces (e.g., "risk-assessment indicators") to speed future overlap checks.

Freshness and revision-risk tags (retrieval optimization)

Use these compact tags in internal triage notes to avoid over-trusting revision-sensitive feeds.

  • F0 (live/operational): minute/hourly feeds; high utility for breaking checks, high volatility risk.
  • F1 (daily/near-daily): strong short-cycle monitoring value; expect occasional backfills.
  • F2 (periodic official): weekly/monthly official releases; stable baseline, slower signal.
  • F3 (structural/annual): long-horizon context only; unsuitable for real-time claims.

Revision flags: - R+ (revision-prone): source commonly backfills or restates recent periods. - R0 (low revision): revision risk present but typically limited after release cutoffs.

Operational rule: for high-impact claims, avoid single-source conclusions from F0+R+ datasets without at least one independent corroboration family.

Country-shard dedup protocol (DATASETS_OPTIMIZE)

  • If many country variants of the same dataset family appear in discovery, add only shards that improve geographic coverage gaps for current monitoring priorities.
  • Avoid adding adjacent-country shards solely due to recency timestamps when they do not expand decision-relevant coverage.
  • In traces, list selected shards and at least one intentionally skipped shard family to document breadth-vs-duplication judgment.

Municipal-feed comparability protocol (DATASETS_OPTIMIZE)

  • Treat cross-city crime and crash feeds as within-city trend tools first, not direct city-vs-city ranking tools.
  • When used for cross-city context, require a same-period normalization note (population, reporting standard, or offense definition scope).
  • If a municipal dataset lacks stable definition history, cap confidence for comparative claims and document the missing standardization artifact.

Conflict, unrest, and information control

  • ACLED — Near-real-time conflict and protest event data. Strong for intensity/trajectory checks, but regional reporting lag and source bias require triangulation.
  • UCDP GED — Curated historical conflict events. Best for baseline and long-window comparison, not immediate same-day claims.
  • ICEWS — Machine-coded actor/event dataset useful for interaction trend shifts. Treat as signal-heavy and media-dependent, not ground truth.
  • GDELT — Global event/media coding for fast anomaly detection and narrative-shift tracking. Excellent early warning, weaker as final evidentiary anchor.
  • OONI — Network interference and censorship measurements. Useful for shutdown/censorship corroboration, with coverage uneven by probe geography.
  • RIPE Atlas — Distributed active internet measurements for reachability/latency checks. Good for disruption diagnostics, limited by probe placement.
  • CAIDA IODA — Outage detection via BGP/darknet/active signals. Strong for macro outage events, less sensitive to app/platform-level blocking.

Disaster response, recovery, and resilience finance

  • Disaster Declarations Summaries — FEMA registry of federally declared disasters (major disaster, emergency, fire management assistance) since 1953, including declaration timing and designated geographies. High-value trigger dataset for hazard exposure and federal-response activation baselines, with declaration-policy and backfill caveats.
  • Individuals and Households Program (IHP) – Valid Registrations — County/city/ZIP-level non-PII FEMA applicant records for household disaster-assistance demand. Useful near-operational displacement and household-loss pressure signal, with eligibility-rule and reporting-lag caveats.
  • Public Assistance Funded Projects Details — FEMA obligated project-level public-assistance records for debris removal, emergency protective measures, and infrastructure restoration support. Strong post-disaster recovery-finance signal, with unobligated-project blind spots and obligation-timing caveats.
  • Hazard Mitigation Assistance Projects — Funded mitigation-planning and resilience projects under FEMA HMA programs, useful for tracking where future-loss reduction investments are actually landing. Decision-useful adaptation baseline with award-cycle and program-rule caveats.
  • Housing Assistance Program Data – Renters — Aggregated non-PII FEMA renter-assistance registrations by geography for declared disasters, useful for housing-displacement pressure tracking and renter-specific recovery burden analysis. High social-impact signal with eligibility-rule and declaration-scope caveats.
  • Housing Assistance Program Data – Owners — Aggregated non-PII FEMA housing-assistance records for owner-occupied households (state/county/ZIP) after declared disasters, useful for tracking owner-side housing-loss burden and recovery demand. High decision value for housing recovery triage, with self-reported geography and financial-reporting caveats. Metadata updated: 2025-06-07.
  • FEMA Web Disaster Declarations — FEMA declaration-type and authorized-assistance dataset that helps confirm when and where federal disaster authorities are activated. Strong trigger and scope baseline for response reporting, with historical backfill and raw-entry caveats. Metadata updated: 2025-06-07.
  • FEMA Web Disaster Summaries — Disaster-level FEMA summary table with approved applications and assistance amounts across individual, public, and mitigation channels. Useful high-level severity and support-flow context, with non-official-financial-reporting caveats. Metadata updated: 2025-06-07.
  • Hazard Mitigation Grant Program - Disaster Summaries — Disaster-level Hazard Mitigation Grant Program financial summaries for tracking post-disaster resilience-investment commitments. High-value adaptation-finance signal with known reporting-period and business-rule caveats versus official systems. Metadata updated: 2025-06-07.
  • Public Assistance Funded Project Summaries — FEMA summary records of Public Assistance applicants and funded support for debris removal, emergency protective work, and infrastructure restoration. Useful recovery-finance breadth indicator with raw-system and official-report divergence caveats. Metadata updated: 2025-08-11.
  • Registration Intake and Individuals Household Program (RI-IHP) — Aggregated FEMA registration and Individuals and Households Program intake summaries that provide early demand-side volume signals after disasters. Operationally useful intake gauge with reporting-workflow and lag caveats. Metadata updated: 2025-06-07.
  • Individual Assistance Housing Registrants – Large Disasters — FEMA housing registrant counts focused on large disasters, supporting severity benchmarking and concentration analysis across major events. Useful high-consequence disaster-comparison layer with threshold-definition and comparability caveats.
  • FEMA Individual Assistance Open Disaster Statistics — Open-disaster assistance statistics for ongoing declarations, supporting near-operational tracking of applicant volume, approval progression, and funding context while incidents remain active. Decision-relevant ongoing-response feed with status-change and update-lag caveats.
  • National Risk Index (NRI) Data — FEMA county- and census-tract risk baseline combining expected annual losses across multiple natural hazards with social vulnerability and community resilience fields. High-value preparedness denominator for prioritizing where hazard exposure and limited resilience overlap, with model-assumption and aggregation caveats. Metadata updated: 2025-07-06.
  • Hazard Mitigation Plan Statuses (OpenFEMA) — FEMA mitigation-planning status records showing participating jurisdictions, approval timelines, and expiration windows for hazard mitigation plans. Operational governance signal for tracking preparedness readiness and grant-eligibility posture, with regional-entry lag and human-error caveats. Metadata updated: 2025-02-10.
  • NFIP Multiple Loss Properties — FEMA flood-insurance structures with repeated claims, including repetitive/severe repetitive loss categories and mitigation status flags for identifying where flood damage recurs and where mitigation has or has not reduced exposure. High-consequence household-risk and insurance-pressure baseline with location redaction/privacy constraints and address-quality caveats. Metadata updated: 2025-06-07.
  • FIMA NFIP Redacted Claims (OpenFEMA) — Redacted National Flood Insurance Program claims transactions from OpenFEMA for direct claims-flow and loss-pattern monitoring across time. Strong household-loss and insurer-exposure signal with privacy redactions, historical-record quality variation, and revision-process caveats. Metadata updated: 2025-09-08.
  • FIMA NFIP Redacted Policies (OpenFEMA) — Large-scale redacted NFIP policy transaction records (monthly-updated with lag) that expose policy take-up, renewals, and coverage-structure dynamics across flood-exposed communities. Decision-useful insurance-coverage baseline with record-size tooling constraints, redaction limits, and non-official-report caveats. Metadata updated: 2025-09-08.
