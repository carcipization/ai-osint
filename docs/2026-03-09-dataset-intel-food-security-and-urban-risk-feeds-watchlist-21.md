## What changed

This DATASETS_A cycle adds five catalog sources:

1. **WFP Global Food Prices**
2. **FAO Data in Emergencies (DIEM)**
3. **Harmonized Tariff Schedule of the United States (2026)**
4. **NYC Air Quality**
5. **NYC Motor Vehicle Collisions – Crashes**

## Why these additions

- **WFP Global Food Prices** improves cross-country inflation and food-stress corroboration for humanitarian reporting.
- **FAO DIEM** adds structured crisis household-survey evidence for livelihoods and food-security deterioration checks.
- **U.S. Harmonized Tariff Schedule** strengthens trade-policy and duty-change verification using an official tariff baseline.
- **NYC Air Quality** adds high-frequency urban pollution context for local health/hazard story framing.
- **NYC Motor Vehicle Collisions** improves city-level public-safety monitoring with incident-level crash signals.

Together these additions expand coverage depth in humanitarian/hazard, trade-policy context, and domestic urban risk tracking.
