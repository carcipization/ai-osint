This DATASETS_B cycle adds three country-level humanitarian risk datasets to improve cross-region baseline coverage for consequence-first monitoring.

Added in this batch (3 net-new)

  1. Argentina - Risk Assessment Indicators
    Country-level humanitarian risk indicators for exposure, vulnerability, and response-capacity context in the Southern Cone.

  2. Colombia - Risk Assessment Indicators
    Structured risk indicators supporting displacement, hazard, and service-access baseline checks in northern South America.

  3. Viet Nam - Risk Assessment Indicators
    National humanitarian risk indicators useful for climate and service-disruption consequence screening in Southeast Asia.

Why this matters now

These additions broaden denominator coverage across Latin America and Southeast Asia while keeping a consistent indicator family that is fast to compare under time pressure. That improves early consequence framing when new conflict, weather, or service-disruption signals emerge.

Practical use cases include: - rapid vulnerability context before elevating story candidates, - cross-country comparisons of likely household-level impact, - and better triage of where preparedness attention is most urgent.
