Datasets: public infrastructure and safety stack expands consequence monitoring

Human-readable HTML: HTML LLM-friendly Markdown: Markdown

Dateline: 2026-03-25 18:24 UTC

This one-off DATASETS run adds six net-new datasets that improve high-impact monitoring of water safety, emergency response coverage, and transport-system reliability.

These datasets were selected for broad non-specialist consequence value: they connect directly to household safety, commute and freight continuity, and local service resilience during disruptions.

Added datasets (6)

  • SDWIS/FED Drinking Water Data
    https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/sdwisfed-drinking-water-data
  • Monthly Modal Time Series
    https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/monthly-modal-time-series
  • National Bridge Inventory
    https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/national-bridge-inventory-abd5a
  • Highway-Rail Accidents
    https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/highway-rail-accidents
  • Train Accident Reports (Form FRA 6180.54)
    https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/train-accident-reports-landing-page
  • Emergency Medical Service (EMS) Stations
    https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/emergency-medical-service-ems-stations

Why this is useful now

  • Households and communities: faster checks on drinking-water compliance risk and emergency-response access.
  • Commuters and operators: stronger visibility into transit demand stress and infrastructure reliability signals.
  • Safety and continuity planning: better linkage between rail incidents, bridge condition context, and downstream service disruptions.

Quick-use pattern

  1. Use SDWIS/FED to identify violation clusters and affected system populations.
  2. Use Monthly Modal Time Series to detect demand shocks or recovery breaks in urban mobility.
  3. Use National Bridge Inventory to map infrastructure vulnerability near critical commuter/freight corridors.
  4. Pair Highway-Rail Accidents with Train Accident Reports to separate crossing incidents from broader rail-equipment safety stress.
  5. Overlay EMS Stations to check emergency-response coverage where transport or hazard stress is rising.

Caveats

  • Several feeds are administrative or inspection-driven, not real-time incident telemetry.
  • Incident counts require denominator context (traffic volume, crossing exposure, network size).
  • Transit and infrastructure series can revise as agencies backfill submissions.
