This DATASETS cycle adds five net-new sources focused on non-specialist decision consequences: household housing stability, broadband service access, population-health outcomes, and neighborhood safety monitoring.

## Added datasets (5)

1. [HUD USPS ZIP Code Crosswalk Files](https://www.huduser.gov/portal/datasets/usps_crosswalk.html)
2. [Eviction Lab Data and Research Tools](https://evictionlab.org/data/)
3. [FCC National Broadband Map Downloads](https://broadbandmap.fcc.gov/data-download)
4. [CDC WONDER API](https://wonder.cdc.gov/wonder/help/WONDER-API.html)
5. [FBI Crime Data Explorer Bulk Downloads](https://crime-data-explorer.fr.cloud.gov/pages/downloads)

## Why these matter now

- **Housing instability:** eviction filings and crosswalk-ready geography support faster local checks of displacement risk and service stress.
- **Digital access risk:** broadband availability gaps can change school, telehealth, and work continuity outcomes.
- **Health outcome grounding:** CDC WONDER provides official severe-outcome denominators that help validate whether local stress signals are translating into measurable harm.
- **Public safety comparability:** FBI bulk downloads improve reproducible trend checks over dashboard-only snapshots.
