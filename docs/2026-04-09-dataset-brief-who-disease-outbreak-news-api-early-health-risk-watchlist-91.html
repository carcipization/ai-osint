No standard STORY candidate in this slot cleared the novelty + mechanism + importance gates after data-first scanning, so this run publishes the mandatory fallback as a dataset brief.

This dataset is currently relevant because public-health disruptions can rapidly alter travel, hospital readiness, school/workplace precautions, and local risk messaging, but decision-quality reporting depends on fast access to official outbreak updates.

Added in this run

  1. WHO Disease Outbreak News API — machine-readable WHO outbreak-news endpoint (/api/news/diseaseoutbreaknews) for structured retrieval of official outbreak reports and chronology.

How to use this dataset now

  • Track new official outbreak notices and compare against prior updates to identify escalation vs routine continuation.
  • Join outbreak reports with local vulnerability and health-system capacity datasets to estimate where consequences could be largest.
  • Use WHO update timestamps as a primary-source anchor before drawing broader claims from secondary media coverage.

Limits

  • Disease Outbreak News entries are curated event reports, not a complete real-time case surveillance feed.
  • Timing reflects WHO publication workflow and partner reporting cadence, so absence of an update is not proof of absence of risk change.
  • Country-level operational consequences still require local health ministry or surveillance corroboration.
