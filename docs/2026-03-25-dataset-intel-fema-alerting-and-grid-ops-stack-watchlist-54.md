This DATASETS_A run adds four machine-readable sources that strengthen near-operational coverage of disaster household impact, emergency public warning trails, and electric-grid stress context.

## Added datasets (new this run)

1. **EIA Real-time Operating Grid Monitor**  
   Why it matters now: gives near-real-time balancing-authority demand and generation context for checking whether fuel and transmission shocks are spilling into service reliability risk.

2. **Housing Assistance Program Data - Renters - v2 (OpenFEMA)**  
   Why it matters now: improves renter-side disaster burden tracking with a current OpenFEMA schema, helping separate household displacement pressure from owner-focused recovery patterns.

3. **Individuals and Households Program - Valid Registrations - v2 (OpenFEMA)**  
   Why it matters now: provides fast applicant-volume signal after declarations, useful for measuring where household assistance demand is accelerating before slower recovery statistics finalize.

4. **OpenFEMA Dataset: IPAWS Archived Alerts - v1**  
   Why it matters now: enables timing and geography checks on public emergency warnings, which is critical for validating response sequencing and communication gaps during active hazard windows.

## Selection notes

- **Consequence-first:** prioritized datasets with direct links to household recovery demand, public alert timing, and service reliability.
- **Decision utility:** selected feeds that can change actions for emergency managers, local operators, and risk-monitoring users within hours to days.
- **Overlap check:** all four entries are net-new additions to the current catalog (adjacent to existing FEMA/EIA family coverage, but non-duplicate dataset endpoints).
