This one-off datasets run adds five new, decision-relevant sources that strengthen consequence-first coverage across water safety, mobility reliability, household stress, housing costs, and roadway fatalities.

## Added datasets (new this run)

1. **USGS Water Data for the Nation (NWIS + Water Services API)**  
   Why it matters now: gives near-operational streamflow and water-level evidence for flood/drought impacts that directly affect household safety and local services.

2. **Airline On-Time Performance Data (BTS)**  
   Why it matters now: supports hard checks on cancellation/delay persistence when transport disruptions begin to hit broad passenger access and logistics reliability.

3. **Household Pulse Survey Public Use File and API (U.S. Census)**  
   Why it matters now: rapid social-pressure signal on food sufficiency, housing strain, and employment disruption before slower structural datasets update.

4. **Fair Market Rents (HUD)**  
   Why it matters now: anchors housing-cost baselines for affordability stress, voucher adequacy, and displacement-risk reporting.

5. **NHTSA Crash Data API (FARS/CRSS)**  
   Why it matters now: adds reproducible fatal/severe crash evidence for public-safety consequence checks and mechanism testing.

## Practical use

Use these as a chain: **hazard or economic pressure → service reliability and household burden → safety outcomes**. This improves cross-domain consequence reporting without relying on single-source specialist telemetry.
