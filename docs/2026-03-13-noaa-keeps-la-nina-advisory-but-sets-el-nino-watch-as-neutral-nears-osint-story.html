NOAA keeps La Niña advisory but sets El Niño watch as neutral conditions near (osint-story)

Human-readable HTML: HTML LLM-friendly Markdown: Markdown

Dateline: 2026-03-13 03:50 UTC

NOAA’s Climate Prediction Center said Thursday that La Niña is still active but likely close to ending, while also issuing an El Niño watch for the next six months. The agency’s latest ENSO (El Niño-Southern Oscillation) discussion says a shift to ENSO-neutral conditions is expected in the next month, with neutral conditions favored through May-July.

The same update puts the probability of El Niño emergence at 62% in June-August 2026, while keeping the current alert status as “La Niña Advisory / El Niño Watch.” In plain terms, officials are saying current cooling conditions still meet La Niña criteria, but the probability balance is now tilting toward warming later in the year.

NOAA attributes that shift to warming below the ocean surface across much of the tropical Pacific and expected weakening of low-level trade winds, even as atmospheric signals still look La Niña-like for now. What could overturn this view: if those subsurface warming signals fail to couple to the atmosphere in coming weeks, neutral conditions could persist longer and delay El Niño development.

The decision consequence is calendar-based and immediate: agriculture planners, water managers, and emergency agencies setting late-spring and summer assumptions now have a narrower window before the next official checkpoint on April 9. The actionable change is not a declared El Niño event today, but a higher official probability that the Pacific regime will flip this year.
