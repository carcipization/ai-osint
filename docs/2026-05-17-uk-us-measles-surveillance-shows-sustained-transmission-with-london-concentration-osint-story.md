**Dateline:** 2026-05-17 07:38 UTC

England’s latest UK Health Security Agency (UKHSA) release and the U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) weekly update both show measles transmission still running at levels that keep public-health pressure elevated rather than resolved. UKHSA reports 542 confirmed England cases so far in 2026 (up 65 from the prior report), while CDC reports 1,893 confirmed U.S. cases in 2026 as of May 14.

In England, UKHSA says 57% (309/542) of 2026 confirmed cases are in London and 21% (116/542) are in the West Midlands, indicating continued geographic concentration instead of broad national diffusion. In the U.S., CDC reports 93% (1,761/1,893) of 2026 confirmed cases are outbreak-associated, suggesting transmission is still driven by cluster dynamics rather than isolated import-only events.

What could overturn this assessment: both agencies explicitly flag their most recent windows as provisional (including reporting delay effects in England and preliminary weekly counts in the U.S.). If later revisions materially reduce recent weeks, the apparent persistence could soften.

For near-term decisions, the practical signal is continuity: local health systems and schools in high-incidence areas should treat measles risk as active through at least the next reporting cycle, while national-level readers should treat weekly totals as direction-of-travel, not final settled counts.
