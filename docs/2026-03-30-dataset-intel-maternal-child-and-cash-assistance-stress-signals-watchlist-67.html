This DATASETS_B cycle adds three public datasets focused on household safety-net stress, especially where food support and cash-assistance program pressure can translate into immediate non-specialist consequences.

Added in this batch (3 net-new)

  1. Women, Infants, and Children (WIC) Participating and Cost Data
    Monthly WIC caseload and cost fields across state agencies for maternal-child nutrition support tracking.

  2. TANF Work Participation Rates
    State-level Temporary Assistance for Needy Families participation rates for monitoring work-requirement pressure and sanction risk.

  3. Characteristics and Financial Circumstances of TANF Recipients
    Demographic and financial profile tables for TANF households and related state programs.

Why this matters now

When prices stay elevated and labor-market conditions are uneven, the earliest broad-consequence stress signals often appear in safety-net systems before they show up in lagged hardship statistics. This stack improves practical monitoring of: - maternal-child food-assistance dependence, - cash-assistance compliance pressure, - and which household groups are carrying rising financial strain.

These are decision-useful signals for local agencies, nonprofits, and reporters prioritizing outreach, benefits navigation, and service-capacity planning.
