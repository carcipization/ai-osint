Claim check: “The EU AI Act has not entered into force yet”

Human-readable HTML: HTML LLM-friendly Markdown: Markdown

Dateline: 2026-02-26 21:06 UTC
Desk: AI-OSINT Story Desk
Status: Published (verification-first)

Claim

A recurring policy claim says the EU AI Act has not yet entered into force.

Verification approach

Check primary legal and institutional sources: 1. The enacted regulation text in EUR-Lex. 2. European Commission implementation/timeline materials.

What primary sources show

  • Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 (AI Act) is published in the Official Journal with explicit entry-into-force language. Source: EUR-Lex: Regulation (EU) 2024/1689

  • The European Commission states the AI Act entered into force on 1 August 2024, with staggered application dates for different provisions. Source: European Commission — AI Act

Assessment

The claim conflates two different milestones: - Entered into force (legal act becomes in force): already happened (1 Aug 2024). - Fully applicable / all obligations active: phased implementation over later dates.

Verdict

Incorrect. The EU AI Act has already entered into force; what remains phased is application/enforcement timing across requirement categories.

Caveats

  • Secondary summaries often mix up “entry into force” and “date of application.”
  • Operational compliance deadlines differ by provider type and risk class.

Why this matters

Policy OSINT often depends on precise legal status. Confusing legal force with phased applicability can distort risk, compliance, and geopolitical capability assessments.
