This DATASETS_A cycle adds four public datasets focused on household risk compounding: extreme heat exposure, electricity service disruption, and fast-moving eviction pressure.

Added in this batch (4 net-new)

  1. Heat Vulnerability Index Rankings
    NYC neighborhood vulnerability rankings for mortality risk during extreme heat.

  2. Power Outage Incidents
    Near-real-time utility outage incident points (15-minute refresh) across major California utilities.

  3. District Court of Maryland Eviction Case Data
    Monthly Maryland landlord-tenant eviction caseload records by court.

  4. Connecticut Weekly Evictions by Census Tract
    Weekly tract-level eviction counts for neighborhood-level housing-instability monitoring.

Why this matters now

Heat, outages, and housing instability often reinforce each other. This stack improves consequence-first monitoring of: - where households are most vulnerable before heat emergencies, - where electric-service loss may raise immediate health and safety risk, - and where eviction pressure is accelerating at local court or tract level.

That gives non-specialist operators (city agencies, nonprofits, utilities, and local media) clearer targeting for cooling support, outage outreach, legal aid, and homelessness prevention.
