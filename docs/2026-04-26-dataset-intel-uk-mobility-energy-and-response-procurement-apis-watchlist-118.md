This DATASETS cycle adds four net-new, direct-access sources focused on broad non-specialist consequences: commuter reliability, weather-linked disruption risk, power-system stress context, and emergency procurement visibility.

## Added datasets (4)

1. [Transport for London (TfL) Unified API](https://api-portal.tfl.gov.uk/)
2. [UK Carbon Intensity API](https://api.carbonintensity.org.uk/)
3. [Met Office DataPoint](https://www.metoffice.gov.uk/services/data/datapoint)
4. [SAM.gov Get Opportunities Public API](https://open.gsa.gov/api/get-opportunities-public-api/)

## Why these matter now

- **Commuter and service reliability:** TfL status/arrival/disruption endpoints support rapid checks of urban mobility stress affecting households and workers.
- **Energy-system pressure context:** UK Carbon Intensity adds short-cycle, machine-readable grid condition context that can be paired with demand/outage datasets in consequence reporting.
- **Public safety weather signal:** Met Office DataPoint improves direct weather observation/forecast pulls for hazard and service-disruption corroboration.
- **Emergency operations and contracting transparency:** SAM opportunities data helps track whether procurement activity is shifting toward response logistics and recovery services.
