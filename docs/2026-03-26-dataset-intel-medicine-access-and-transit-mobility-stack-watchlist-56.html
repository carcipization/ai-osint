Dateline: 2026-03-26 17:42 UTC

This DATASETS_A run adds six net-new datasets that strengthen consequence-first coverage around two public-facing decision surfaces: (1) medication access and affordability continuity, and (2) everyday urban mobility access through transit mode splits.

Added datasets (new this run)

  1. Current and Resolved Drug Shortages and Discontinuations Reported to FDA
    Why it matters now: provides near-operational shortage/discontinuation visibility that can surface treatment access risk before downstream hospitalization signals.

  2. Monthly Prescription Drug Plan Formulary and Pharmacy Network Information
    Why it matters now: captures monthly plan-level formulary/network availability shifts that directly affect patient refill pathways and out-of-pocket burden.

  3. Hospitals (HIFLD)
    Why it matters now: adds a national hospital-facility geography layer for mapping healthcare access exposure during localized outages, disasters, or transport disruptions.

  4. Transit Ridership - Urban Rail
    Why it matters now: enables mode-specific monitoring of metro rail access pressure where commuting and service access depend on rail throughput.

  5. Transit Ridership - Fixed Route Bus
    Why it matters now: tracks bus-network demand stress in communities where buses are the primary low-cost mobility channel.

  6. Transit Ridership - Other Transit Modes
    Why it matters now: adds demand-response/ferry/vanpool visibility to catch mobility stress in areas with weak fixed-route coverage.

Selection notes

  • Batch rule satisfied: 6 additions (required 3–10).
  • Overlap pass: all promoted entries classified as net-new versus current catalog.
  • Breadth-first outcome: additions intentionally span healthcare access and transit mobility consequences rather than deepening one existing narrow telemetry cluster.
