ECDC reclassifies Andes cluster to include one inconclusive case without new growth

Dateline: 2026-05-13 20:24 UTC

ECDC’s 13 May update keeps the overall Andes-virus cluster total at 11 but changes the composition to 8 confirmed, 2 probable, and 1 inconclusive, while explicitly stating no new cases or deaths since the previous update. The practical signal is classification refinement, not acceleration.

What changed

Compared with prior public snapshots centered on confirmed/probable buckets, ECDC now surfaces an explicit “inconclusive” category while maintaining the same top-line total and reporting no fresh growth.

Evidence

  1. ECDC outbreak update (13 May 2026) (retrieved 2026-05-13 20:24 UTC): reports 11 total cases (8 confirmed, 2 probable, 1 inconclusive), no new cases/deaths, repatriation completed, and very low risk to the EU/EEA general population.
  2. CDC situation summary (retrieved 2026-05-13 20:24 UTC): still reports no confirmed U.S. outbreak-linked cases and extremely low risk to U.S. public/travelers.
  3. WHO DON600 (retrieved 2026-05-13 20:24 UTC): latest published DON remains an earlier 8-case snapshot with low global-risk framing.

Hypotheses and adjudication

  • H1 (supported): Current agency signals are consistent with data reconciliation and case-status refinement rather than outbreak reacceleration.
  • H2 (supported): Population-level risk framing remains low/very low across ECDC, CDC, and WHO.
  • H3 (open): WHO may revise published counts in a future DON to align with later regional reporting.

Limitations

  • Public reporting cadences are asynchronous across WHO, ECDC, and CDC.
  • “Inconclusive” classification criteria are not fully line-listed in public summaries.

Confidence

High that the latest ECDC update reflects case-mix reclassification without top-line growth and High that broad public-risk posture remains low.

Why it matters

Operationally, this favors steady monitoring and contact-management execution over headline escalation: denominator quality improved, but transmission-risk posture did not materially worsen.
