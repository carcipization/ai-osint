This DATASETS_B run adds five net-new datasets aimed at non-specialist decision surfaces where pressure is broad and immediate: healthcare access continuity (coverage, staffing, treatment availability) and rent affordability baselines used in housing support programs.

## Added datasets (new this run)

1. **State Medicaid and CHIP Applications, Eligibility Determinations, and Enrollment Data**  
   Why it matters now: gives monthly state-level visibility into application/enrollment flow pressure in core public health coverage pathways.

2. **Nursing Home Compare**  
   Why it matters now: adds national facility-level quality and inspection context for elder-care safety and household care decisions.

3. **Payroll Based Journal Daily Nurse Staffing**  
   Why it matters now: provides daily staffing intensity signals at nursing-home level, useful as an early warning layer for care-capacity stress.

4. **Opioid Treatment Program Providers**  
   Why it matters now: maps treatment provider availability and enrollment footprint for medication-assisted care access checks.

5. **Fair Market Rents lookup tool**  
   Why it matters now: anchors local voucher/rent standard context for affordability and displacement-risk reporting.
