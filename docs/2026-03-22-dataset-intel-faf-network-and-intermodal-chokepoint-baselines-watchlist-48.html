Datasets: FAF network and intermodal chokepoint baselines expanded (Watchlist 48)

Human-readable HTML: HTML LLM-friendly Markdown: Markdown

Dateline: 2026-03-22 17:41 UTC

This datasets-only cycle adds four freight-network and transfer-point baselines that improve corridor-level tracking of how maritime and fuel disruptions can propagate into inland logistics bottlenecks.

Added datasets (4)

  1. Freight Analysis Framework (FAF5) Network Links
    URL: https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/freight-analysis-framework-faf5-network-links1
    Why it matters: Adds connected corridor geometry for tracing where freight rerouting pressure accumulates across the highway freight network.

  2. Freight Analysis Framework (FAF5) Network Nodes
    URL: https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/freight-analysis-framework-faf5-network-nodes1
    Why it matters: Adds network junction topology needed to identify likely bottleneck concentration points during route shifts.

  3. Intermodal Freight Facilities
    URL: https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/intermodal-freight-facilities
    Why it matters: Adds transfer/transloading points across truck-rail-air-marine-pipeline interfaces, improving mode-switch friction monitoring.

  4. Freight Facts and Figures
    URL: https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/freight-facts-and-figures
    Why it matters: Adds a concise national performance denominator for translating specialized freight signals into public-facing context.

Decision-use framing

  • Logistics operators: prioritize contingency plans around high-density FAF nodes and intermodal transfer clusters.
  • Local/state planners: map corridor stress where maritime delays could spill into inland trucking and rail handoffs.
  • General audience reporting: separate structural freight-network exposure from daily market noise.

Method notes

  • Slot policy: DATASETS_A (datasets-only), required batch size met (4 additions).
  • Selection prioritized broad non-specialist consequence (prices, availability, delivery reliability), cross-domain chain value, and complementarity with existing freight/port datasets.
  • Metadata recency from catalog pages: FAF5 Links (2025-07-17), FAF5 Nodes (2025-07-17), Intermodal Freight Facilities (2025-07-17), Freight Facts and Figures (2023-12-07).
