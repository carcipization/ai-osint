This DATASETS_A cycle adds three country-level humanitarian risk datasets to widen cross-region baseline coverage for high-population consequence screening.

## Added in this batch (3 net-new)

1. **Brazil - Risk Assessment Indicators**  
   Country-level risk indicators for exposure, vulnerability, and coping-capacity context in Latin America’s largest population center.

2. **Egypt - Risk Assessment Indicators**  
   Structured humanitarian risk indicators for baseline checks on hazard exposure, service pressure, and resilience context in North Africa.

3. **South Africa - Risk Assessment Indicators**  
   National humanitarian risk indicators supporting preparedness and social-stress context checks in Southern Africa.

## Why this matters now

These additions improve breadth across Latin America and Africa while preserving a consistent, comparable risk-indicator family. That improves rapid denominator checks when disruption signals appear and helps tie headline events to likely household-level consequences.

Practical uses include:
- faster consequence framing for emerging hazard and service-disruption stories,
- cross-country vulnerability context before elevating claims,
- and better prioritization of where access/safety stress may intensify.
