Dataset brief

Dataset: Subsidy Uptake Dashboard (Washington State)

Source URL: https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/subsidy-take-up-dashboard

This dataset tracks estimated take-up of childcare subsidies among income-eligible families, including age-group participation splits (infants, toddlers, preschool-age, school-age). It is built as a practical monitoring layer for access gaps in subsidized childcare.

Why this is high-consequence

Childcare access is a direct household stability issue, not a specialist-only metric. When subsidy uptake stays low relative to eligibility, families can face immediate constraints on:

  • ability to keep regular work hours,
  • household income continuity,
  • children’s care arrangements and routines,
  • demand pressure on local social services.

In short: this is an early-warning signal for family economic stress that can propagate quickly into labor participation and local hardship support demand.

How to use it

Useful checks include: - trend in subsidy uptake share over time, - age-band divergence (e.g., infant vs preschool take-up), - county or local concentration of low uptake, - cross-checks with unemployment, rent-arrears, and emergency-assistance series.

Scope and caveats

  • State-specific scope (Washington), so not a national denominator by itself.
  • Eligibility is model-estimated; interpretation should account for method assumptions.
  • Best used as a decision-support signal alongside other hardship and labor datasets.
