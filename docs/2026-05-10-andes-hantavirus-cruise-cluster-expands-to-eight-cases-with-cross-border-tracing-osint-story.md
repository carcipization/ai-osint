## What changed, and why now
WHO’s updated outbreak bulletin reports that the cruise-linked Andes virus cluster increased to **eight total cases (six confirmed, two probable) and three deaths** as of 8 May, with patients dispersed across multiple countries and international contact tracing underway. CDC simultaneously issued a Health Alert Network advisory and public situation update, indicating active U.S. response coordination while keeping overall U.S. public risk assessed as very low.

## Why this matters
If true, this event is a practical stress test for cross-border outbreak control in travel settings where severe disease has high fatality risk and person-to-person transmission is possible with close contact. If false (or largely a classification artifact), authorities could over-allocate scarce response resources while missing higher-priority outbreaks.

## Hypotheses and adjudication
- **H1 (supported):** Primary spillover occurred before/early in voyage, then limited onward transmission happened in close-contact onboard/travel contexts.  
  - Prediction: cases should cluster within known incubation windows and close-contact chains; WHO describes this pattern and ongoing source investigation.
- **H2 (mixed):** Sustained and broad person-to-person transmission onboard is the dominant mechanism.  
  - Prediction: rapidly accelerating, geographically diffuse secondary chains beyond close contacts; current data shows spread across countries but not yet broad uncontrolled transmission.
- **H3 (weakly supported, null/artifact):** Apparent growth is mostly testing/classification artifact.  
  - Prediction: substantial case downgrades and little true net growth; one prior suspect was reclassified non-case, but net confirmed burden still increased.

## Limitations
- Outbreak investigation is live; case classifications can change with delayed PCR/serology and sequencing updates.
- Public reports do not yet provide full exposure-network detail for all passengers and crew.
- Country-level downstream secondary transmission counts are not yet fully published.

## Confidence
**Medium.** Core facts (case count increase, deaths, Andes confirmation, official risk posture) are corroborated by WHO and CDC. Confidence is not high because source attribution and transmission-chain completeness remain under investigation.

## Decision relevance (one line)
Public-health teams and travel medicine providers should maintain targeted screening/monitoring for recent close-contact cruise exposures, while avoiding broad-travel overreaction given currently low population-level risk.
