This DATASETS_A run adds six net-new datasets that strengthen near-real-time visibility across respiratory mortality burden, hospitalization severity, and urban traffic-injury risk in two major U.S. cities.

Added in this batch (6 net-new)

  1. Provisional COVID-19 Death Counts by Week Ending Date and State
    Weekly state-level deaths involving COVID-19, pneumonia, and influenza for fast burden tracking and surge detection.

  2. Provisional COVID-19 death counts, rates, and percent of total deaths, by jurisdiction of residence
    Jurisdiction-level rates and mortality-share views that help separate absolute death burden from composition shifts.

  3. Monthly Rates of Laboratory-Confirmed COVID-19 Hospitalizations from the COVID-NET Surveillance System
    Population-based hospitalization severity signal for evaluating pressure on care systems beyond raw case counts.

  4. Transportation Economic Trends (TET) data
    Cross-series transportation-economy indicators linking freight/passenger movement with broader economic consequence context.

  5. Motor Vehicle Collisions - Crashes (NYC)
    High-frequency police-reported crash-event data for emergency-load and neighborhood roadway risk monitoring.

  6. Traffic Crashes - Crashes (Chicago)
    Citywide crash-event records from CPD reporting to monitor traffic injury/fatality burden and localized safety shifts.

Why this matters now

This stack supports a practical chain of consequences: respiratory burden and hospitalization severity -> care-system pressure and household risk -> transport safety and emergency-service load in dense metro areas.

For public-health operators and city planners, these additions improve early warning on where mortality or hospitalization stress is rising and where transport-safety pressure may compound emergency demand.
