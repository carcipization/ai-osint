This fallback dataset brief adds three energy datasets to improve consequence-first monitoring during continued oil and shipping disruption volatility.

The practical value is decision support: these sources help identify where fuel and power cost shocks are most likely to hit households, transport networks, and local service reliability.

## Added in this run

1. **State Energy Data System (SEDS)** — long-run state-level production, consumption, expenditure, and price baselines.
2. **City and County Energy Profiles** — local-area energy demand/fuel-mix context for county and metro exposure mapping.
3. **Monthly and Annual Energy Consumption by Sector** — sector-level demand shifts (residential/commercial/industrial/transport) for pass-through analysis.

## How to use this stack now

- Use **SEDS** to rank structural state exposure to prolonged fuel-cost shocks.
- Use **City and County Energy Profiles** to localize consequences for non-specialist decisions (household bills, transit reliability, municipal operations).
- Use **sector consumption series** to test which demand segments are absorbing shock first and where policy or procurement response is most urgent.

## Limits

- SEDS and many local profile fields are not real-time; they are denominator and exposure tools, not instant event detectors.
- Sector series can revise and should be interpreted with seasonal context.
- These datasets should be paired with short-cycle operational signals (ports, prices, outages) before causal claims.
