This DATASETS cycle adds four net-new, direct-access transit and emergency-care datasets aimed at broadly felt consequences: missed commutes, service reliability degradation, and ambulance-to-hospital transfer bottlenecks.

## Added datasets (4)

1. [MTA LIRR Delay Verification: Beginning 2010](https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/mta-lirr-delay-verification-beginning-2010)
2. [MTA Metro-North Delay Verification: Beginning 2012](https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/mta-metro-north-delay-verification-beginning-2012)
3. [MTA Subway Delay-Causing Incidents: Beginning 2025](https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/mta-subway-delay-causing-incidents-beginning-2025)
4. [Ambulance Patient Offload Times](https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/ambulance-patient-offload-times)

## Why these matter now

- **Commuter decision utility:** LIRR and Metro-North train-level delay-verification records support sharper checks of real rider-facing reliability loss, not just aggregate punctuality summaries.
- **Failure-mode visibility:** Subway delay-cause breakdowns reveal whether reliability deterioration is broad or concentrated (signal, crew, infrastructure, external incidents), changing what interventions are likely to help.
- **Care-access consequence chain:** Ambulance offload intervals expose emergency department throughput friction that can reduce ambulance availability and lengthen emergency response during demand spikes.
