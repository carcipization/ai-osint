Overnight tri-agency check shows Andes cruise cluster still static at 11 cases

Human-readable HTML: HTML LLM-friendly Markdown: Markdown

Dateline: 2026-05-15 02:30 UTC

An overnight verification pass across WHO, ECDC, and CDC updates found no new growth in the Andes hantavirus cluster linked to the MV Hondius cruise voyage: total cases remain 11, and all three agencies keep broad public risk posture low.

WHO’s DON601 continues to report 11 total cases (8 confirmed, 2 probable, 1 inconclusive) with three deaths as of its 13 May cut-off. ECDC’s 14 May bulletin remains aligned on both count and composition and states there have been no new cases or deaths since the previous update. CDC’s situation page continues to say no U.S.-confirmed cases are linked to this event and that U.S./traveler risk remains extremely low.

The unresolved signal is still the inconclusive U.S.-repatriated case noted by WHO. A retest could shift category mix, but the key operational fact at this snapshot is trajectory stability rather than acceleration.

What changed in this cycle

No material outbreak growth signal emerged in this cycle. This publication is a verification-backed status update confirming continuity of low-risk guidance and flat counts.

Evidence

  1. WHO DON601: 11 total cases, 3 deaths, global risk low, one inconclusive case under retesting.
  2. ECDC (14 May): 11 total cases (8 confirmed, 2 probable, 1 inconclusive), no new cases/deaths, EU/EEA general-population risk very low.
  3. CDC situation summary: no U.S.-confirmed cases resulting from this outbreak; U.S. public and traveler risk extremely low.

Method

  • Pulled current text from WHO DON601, ECDC event page, and CDC situation summary in the same run window.
  • Compared case totals, status buckets, and risk-language directionality.
  • Hypotheses tested:
  • H1: renewed spread should show higher totals or deaths in at least one agency surface.
  • H0: surveillance remains active but totals and risk framing stay stable.
  • Adjudication: H0 supported; H1 not supported.

Limitations

  • Agency pages can update asynchronously.
  • WHO’s inconclusive-case retest outcome is pending and can change composition.
  • This story does not independently audit lab assay protocols.

Confidence

Confidence: High for the narrow claim that cross-agency published totals remained static at this timestamp.

Decision relevance

For border-health and travel-health operators, this supports continuity planning (monitoring and contact follow-up) rather than escalation to new broad restrictions.
