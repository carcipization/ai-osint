This DATASETS cycle adds five net-new, direct-access indicator APIs to strengthen consequence-first coverage across humanitarian service capacity, food affordability stress, and global health baselines.

## Added datasets (5)

1. [HDX HAPI - Coordination & Context: Operational Presence](https://data.humdata.org/dataset/hdx-hapi-operational-presence)
2. [HDX HAPI - Humanitarian Needs: Humanitarian Needs and Response Plan](https://data.humdata.org/dataset/hdx-hapi-humanitarian-needs)
3. [HDX HAPI - Food Security, Nutrition & Poverty: Food Prices](https://data.humdata.org/dataset/hdx-hapi-food-price)
4. [WHO Global Health Observatory (GHO) OData API](https://ghoapi.azureedge.net/api/)
5. [UN SDG API](https://unstats.un.org/SDGAPI/v1/sdg/Goal/List)

## Why these matter now

- **Service-capacity accountability:** Operational-presence data helps test whether aid footprint and sector activity are keeping pace with crisis intensity.
- **Need-versus-response tension:** HNRP indicators provide machine-readable checks on people-in-need and requirement trajectories.
- **Household consequence lens:** HAPI food-price indicators improve cross-country affordability monitoring where inflation and supply disruptions interact.
- **Health-system baseline strength:** WHO GHO adds broader morbidity/mortality/service-context denominators for high-impact health claims.
- **Cross-domain policy comparability:** UN SDG API improves repeatable country baselines across poverty, health, education, water, and infrastructure outcomes.

## Consequence-first screening notes

- All five additions were screened as **net-new** against current catalog URL/title overlap.
- Each source provides direct machine-readable access (API or structured downloadable indicators), not metadata-only directories.
- This batch is intentionally chain-oriented: needs/response capacity + household food stress + health/policy denominator context for stronger future STORY candidate formation.
