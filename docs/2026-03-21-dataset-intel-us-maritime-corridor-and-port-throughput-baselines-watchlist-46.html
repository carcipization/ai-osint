Datasets: U.S. maritime corridor and port-throughput baselines expanded (Watchlist 46)

Human-readable HTML: HTML LLM-friendly Markdown: Markdown

Dateline: 2026-03-21 17:40 UTC

This datasets-only run adds three maritime logistics baselines to improve consequence-first tracking of how global shipping disruptions can propagate into U.S. freight corridors, port concentration risk, and containerized trade flow.

Added datasets (3)

  1. Marine Highways
    URL: https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/marine-highways1
    Why it matters: Maps designated U.S. Marine Highway routes that can absorb pressure when land transport lanes are stressed, giving a concrete routing baseline for contingency freight analysis.

  2. Principal Ports
    URL: https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/principal-ports5
    Why it matters: Adds commodity-tonnage and trade-orientation context (domestic/foreign/import/export) at principal ports, useful for spotting where throughput concentration increases spillover risk under maritime shocks.

  3. Loaded Containers Monthly Imports and Exports Through Port Authority of NY NJ Maritime Terminals: Beginning 2000
    URL: https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/loaded-containers-monthly-imports-and-exports-through-port-authority-of-ny-nj-maritime-ter
    Why it matters: Adds a long-run TEU series for one of the most consequential U.S. container gateways, enabling direct baseline-vs-shock comparison during periods of global route disruption.

Decision-use framing

  • Freight planning: improves visibility on where waterborne alternatives exist when fuel or overland capacity tightens.
  • Chokepoint monitoring: strengthens early warning for concentration risk at high-throughput ports.
  • Household-impact linkage: supports clearer translation from maritime disruption to goods-flow and potential consumer-price pressure.

Method notes

  • Slot policy: DATASETS_A (datasets-only), required batch size met (3 additions).
  • Selection prioritized broad non-specialist consequence potential and cross-domain chain value under current shipping/energy stress context.
  • Metadata recency from catalog pages: Marine Highways (2025-09-11), Principal Ports (2025-12-19), Port NY/NJ loaded containers (2025-07-12).
