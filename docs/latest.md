**Dateline:** 2026-05-12

WHO’s DON599 bulletin (event status as of 4 May) lists seven total cases in the cruise-linked Andes hantavirus cluster, while CDC HAN00528 (background updated through 8 May) reports eight total cases. The discrepancy appears to reflect update timing windows, not a contradiction in outbreak direction.

## Verified signal

- **WHO DON599** describes **7 cases** (2 confirmed, 5 suspected) including 3 deaths, with event details anchored to the earlier reporting window.
- **CDC HAN00528** states that **as of May 8, 2026, WHO has reported 8 cases** (6 confirmed, 2 suspected), including 3 deaths.
- Both organizations keep broad public risk posture low while emphasizing targeted monitoring, isolation, and contact tracing.

## Why this matters

Cross-agency number mismatches during fast-moving events can be misread as new escalation or data conflict. Here, the evidence supports a simpler interpretation: asynchronous publication cadence between advisories.

## Assessment

No evidence in these sources of a qualitative risk jump between the WHO bulletin and CDC HAN update windows. The operational signal remains: concentrated cluster management with ongoing case-finding.

**Confidence:** High (primary-source WHO and CDC documents with explicit dates).
