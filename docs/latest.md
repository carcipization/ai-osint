## What changed
New early-May publication activity across IOM DTM Sudan and UNHCR’s Sudan operational portal indicates that responders are still operating in a high-churn displacement environment, with ongoing snapshot/flash reporting rather than de-escalation-style cadence.

## Why this matters
For humanitarian planners, cadence itself is a signal: when agencies continue issuing rapid snapshots and focused alerts, logistics, shelter, and protection planning should assume sustained pressure rather than near-term normalization.

## Evidence
1. [IOM DTM Sudan portal](https://dtm.iom.int/sudan)  
   Retrieved: 2026-05-11 02:24 UTC. Portal describes Sudan as the world’s largest displacement crisis and shows an IDP monitored indicator (11,560,000), alongside recent displacement/return snapshots and focused flash alerts.
2. [UNHCR Sudan Situation portal](https://data.unhcr.org/en/situations/sudansituation)  
   Retrieved: 2026-05-11 02:24 UTC. Portal lists newly published early-May 2026 monitoring documents (including border/new-arrival updates), consistent with continued cross-border and protection tracking intensity.

## Method
- Queried official agency operational portals for latest publication stream and reporting format.
- Checked whether recent outputs suggest tapering (fewer incident updates, shift to long-interval reporting) versus persistent rapid monitoring.
- Used cross-agency comparison (IOM internal displacement + UNHCR cross-border arrivals/protection stream) to reduce single-source bias.

## Hypotheses and adjudication
- **H1 (supported):** Displacement pressure remains operationally high, requiring frequent monitoring and response updates.  
- **H2 (mixed):** Conditions are stabilizing enough to transition to lower-frequency reporting. Some products are periodic, but current stream still includes frequent operational updates.  
- **H3 (null/artifact, mixed):** Publication frequency reflects communications preference rather than real displacement stress. Possible, but multi-agency continuation of rapid products weakens this explanation.

## Limitations
- Portal extraction is not a full microdata feed; this analysis emphasizes operational reporting cadence and headline indicators rather than a complete event-level re-count.
- Some external pages block automated retrieval, limiting direct pull of all linked annex tables in this run.
- A stronger next pass should ingest downloadable snapshot PDFs directly and compute week-over-week movement deltas.

## Confidence
**Medium-Low.** Directional conclusion (pressure remains elevated) is consistent across IOM/UNHCR operational surfaces, but confidence is constrained by incomplete machine-readable deltas in this run.

## Decision relevance
Treat Sudan-related displacement operations as sustained high-demand in the near term; do not plan around an assumed rapid normalization without fresh numeric downtick evidence.
