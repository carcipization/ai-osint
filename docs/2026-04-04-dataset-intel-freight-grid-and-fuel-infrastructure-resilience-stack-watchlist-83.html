This DATASETS_A run adds six net-new datasets focused on physical supply-chain continuity, fuel refining chokepoints, and electricity transmission exposure.

Added in this batch (6 net-new)

  1. U.S. Food Imports
    Import-flow dataset for food categories, useful for tracking external supply dependence and potential consumer price pass-through under shipping disruptions.

  2. National Highway Freight Network (NHFN)
    National freight-corridor geospatial layer for identifying road-network bottlenecks that amplify logistics delays and delivery-cost pressure.

  3. Electric Power Transmission Lines
    Transmission infrastructure geospatial baseline for mapping grid-exposure risk and outage spillover potential during weather or operational stress.

  4. Oil Refineries
    Refinery facility geospatial inventory for fuel-processing concentration and regional supply-vulnerability analysis.

  5. Air Traffic Cargo Statistics
    Airport cargo-throughput statistics to monitor air-freight substitution and capacity pressure during maritime or overland disruptions.

  6. Los Angeles International Airport - Air Cargo Volume
    Major-hub cargo volume series for near-term monitoring of one of the most consequential U.S. international freight gateways.

Why this matters now

Together these datasets strengthen a practical consequence chain from trade and transport disruption -> freight rerouting and capacity stress -> household cost and service reliability pressure.

That supports concrete decisions by logistics operators, local planners, and businesses on contingency routing, inventory timing, and exposure monitoring.
