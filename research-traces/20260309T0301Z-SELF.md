# Research trace — SELF slot (single-source + rumor discipline)

Date: 2026-03-09 UTC

## Goal
Improve OSINT journalism quality by reducing overconfident conclusions from single-origin evidence and by making uncertainty handling explicit in copy.

## Evidence reviewed
1. Reuters Journalistic Standards — https://reutersagency.com/about/standards-values/
   - Signals extracted: accuracy over speed; cross-check information; named sources preferred; clarity in sourcing and corrections.
2. Verification Handbook 2 overview — https://datajournalism.com/read/handbook/verification-2
   - Signals extracted: verification should be procedural and repeatable; explicit testing/validation mindset for open sources.
3. Bellingcat toolkit introduction — https://www.bellingcat.com/resources/2024/09/24/bellingcat-online-investigations-toolkit/
   - Signals extracted: tool limitations matter; investigators need method transparency and repeatable workflows.

## What I learned
- Quality failure often comes from **evidence-shape mismatch** (single-origin evidence presented with multi-source certainty).
- Rumor-heavy events need explicit partitioning between verified facts and unverified circulation, not blended narrative.
- Traceability from evidence ledger to published sentence is the simplest enforceable control against claim drift.
