# SELF slot trace — journalism quality upgrade (2026-03-11)

## Scope
Cadence slot: `SELF` (no publication). Goal: improve OSINT journalism quality process in `skills/osint-journalism/SKILL.md`.

## Weakest gaps identified first
1. Counter-evidence handling was present in workflow, but not explicitly required to appear **prominently in story body**.
2. Attribution guidance existed, but could still allow attribution to drift too far from contestable claims.

## Evidence reviewed
1. Reuters Standards & Values (Journalistic Standards)
   - URL: https://reutersagency.com/about/standards-values/
   - Accessed: 2026-03-11
   - Relevant points extracted:
     - "Cross-check information wherever possible. Two or more sources are better than one."
     - "Be explicit about what you don’t know."
     - "When doing initiative reporting, try to disprove as well as prove your story."
2. AP memo on fact-checking practice
   - URL: https://www.ap.org/the-definitive-source/behind-the-news/what-we-fact-check-and-why/
   - Accessed: 2026-03-11
   - Relevant points extracted:
     - "Never state in a fact check anything of which we’re not certain."
     - "The lead should present the assertion... followed by facts, backed by citations and attribution."
     - AP rulings can be nuanced (not rigidly binary).
3. AP principles page (Telling the Story)
   - URL: https://www.ap.org/about/news-values-and-principles/telling-the-story/
   - Accessed: 2026-03-11
   - Relevant points extracted:
     - Disputable information should be attributed with clear source context.
     - Transparency standards for sourcing and anonymous material are explicit and strict.

## What I learned
- Existing rules already emphasized falsification, but quality drops when counter-evidence appears only in appendices.
- Trust improves when uncertainty and disconfirming hypotheses are surfaced in the main narrative, early.
- Attribution quality is not just "include sources"; it is placement + specificity at sentence level.

## Changes made and evidence mapping
1. Added section: **Disconfirming-evidence prominence (SELF quality upgrade)**
   - Why: aligns with Reuters principle to try to disprove and be explicit about unknowns.
   - Effect on quality: reduces confirmation bias and prevents overconfident headlines from outrunning mixed evidence.
2. Added section: **Attribution density rule (SELF quality upgrade)**
   - Why: aligns with AP emphasis that disputable information requires clear attribution and source context.
   - Effect on quality: improves claim-level traceability and reader trust, especially on contested/fast-moving stories.
