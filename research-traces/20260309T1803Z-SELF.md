Focus:
Improve verification rigor for UGC/social-claim workflows by reducing origin ambiguity and late-stage contradiction handling.

## What I learned

1) Reuters Journalistic Standards
- Key takeaways: accuracy over speed; cross-check information; be explicit about unknowns; in initiative reporting, try to disprove as well as prove.
- Link: https://reutersagency.com/about/standards-values/

2) Reuters Fact Check methodology
- Key takeaways: identify and summarize key claims; uncover claim foundations by tracing origin; look for both supportive and contrary evidence; provide links.
- Link: https://www.reuters.com/fact-check/about/

3) Bellingcat social verification guide
- Key takeaways: verify originality; assess source credibility/motivation; validate where and when media was captured before accepting claim context.
- Link: https://www.bellingcat.com/resources/2021/11/01/a-beginners-guide-to-social-media-verification/

## Change mapped to evidence

Added "Claim-origin and falsification ladder" to SKILL.md:
- Origin trace first → Reuters Fact Check origin-foundation workflow + Bellingcat originality discipline.
- Claim decomposition into micro-claims → Reuters fact-check style key-claim isolation.
- Counter-evidence sweep → Reuters standard to try disproving initiative reporting.
- Confidence cap when origin unresolved → Reuters sourcing transparency/unknowns discipline.
- Headline fit check → newsroom control to prevent stronger framing than evidence supports.

Could this be wrong because...
- Some fast-breaking events have legitimate origin ambiguity; rigid origin requirements can delay urgent but accurate reporting.
- Micro-claim decomposition may add overhead on low-risk topics; usage should remain proportional to claim criticality.
