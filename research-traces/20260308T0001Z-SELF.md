# Research Trace — SELF

- **UTC window:** 2026-03-08 00:01–00:06 UTC
- **Slot:** SELF
- **Goal:** improve OSINT journalism quality by tightening verification rigor and attribution discipline.

## Weakest gaps identified first

1. Some workflows could still over-weight single-source or single-artifact evidence.
2. UGC (image/video/social) provenance checks were implied but not explicit enough to enforce consistently.
3. Confidence language could drift beyond the strength of directly measured evidence.

## External guidance reviewed (credible)

1. Reuters Journalistic Standards
   - URL: https://reutersagency.com/about/standards-values/
   - Used points: cross-check information, prefer named sources, "try to disprove as well as prove your story," and precision in quoting/attribution.

2. Reuters Fact Check methodology
   - URL: https://www.reuters.com/fact-check/about/
   - Used points: identify claim origins, seek supportive and contradictory evidence, consult multiple sources, publish traceable links.

3. IFCN Code commitments (Poynter)
   - URL: https://ifcncodeofprinciples.poynter.org/the-commitments
   - Used points: transparency of sources, use best available primary sources, and methodology clarity for replication.

4. Bellingcat beginner verification guide
   - URL: https://www.bellingcat.com/resources/2021/11/01/a-beginners-guide-to-social-media-verification/
   - Used points: source/originality/location/time/motivation checks for social content verification.

## What I learned

- The biggest quality lift is not adding more tools; it is forcing explicit evidence independence and falsification checks before narrative lock-in.
- UGC evidence is most error-prone when provenance checks are informal; a minimum ladder (source/content/time/place/integrity) makes weak evidence visible early.
- Attribution wording has to encode evidence strength directly in prose, or confidence drift appears even when appendices are careful.
